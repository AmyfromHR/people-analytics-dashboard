# Meridian Analytics: People Dashboard

A People Analytics dashboard built to show how I think about workforce data as an HR leader. Synthetic data for a fictional 900-employee professional services firm. Three years of history, six departments.

**Live demo:** (https://amy-people-analytics-dashboard.streamlit.app )

---

## What this dashboard demonstrates

Four pieces of analysis I actually use to advise leaders.

### 1. Composite flight risk scoring
Every active employee gets a 0-100 score from six signals: tenure (peak risk 2-4 years), months since last promotion, comp ratio vs. market, recent eNPS, performance trend, and manager change in the last year. The page shows the distribution, departmental concentration, and which drivers dominate. At a glance: is the problem comp, growth, management churn, or engagement?

### 2. Span of control analysis
Histogram of direct reports per manager, with the healthy 3-10 zone highlighted. Surfaces both ends: managers stretched too thin, and managers with too few reports to justify the layer. The conversation to have with a CHRO before a reorg, not after.

### 3. Pay equity (raw gap vs. adjusted gap)
The most common mistake in pay equity reporting: show the raw gender pay gap and stop. A raw gap of -15% might be a level-mix effect (more women in junior roles). A -3% gap elsewhere can hide a real structural problem once you control for level and location. This page shows both numbers side by side per department, so the conversation moves to the **adjusted** gap, the one that actually points to a policy fix.

### 4. Cross-page diagnosis
The pages are designed to be read together. If Marketing's attrition spikes, the eNPS page tells you whether engagement was already cratering. Pay Equity tells you whether comp is part of the story. Flight Risk tells you who's likely to leave next. The synthetic data has these patterns deliberately embedded so the cross-page narrative holds up.

## What the data shows (skim these)

- **Marketing has the worst attrition rate (21%), while Consulting holds the largest concentration of at-risk employees (162 of 378).** Different problems, different interventions: a rate problem in Marketing, a volume problem in Consulting.
- **Mid-career disengagement.** eNPS drops sharply for employees in the 4-6 year tenure band. Classic "stuck in the middle" pattern.
- **Pay equity nuance.** Raw gender gaps look alarming in some departments but compress significantly when adjusted for level and location. In others, the gap persists. That's the real signal.
- **Span of control outliers.** One Consulting manager has 20+ direct reports. Several others have fewer than three.

## How it's built

- **Streamlit** for the multi-page web app
- **Plotly** for interactive charts
- **Pandas / NumPy** for the data layer
- **Python 3.12**
- Synthetic data generated by `data_generator.py` with patterns deliberately embedded

Run locally:
```
pip install -r requirements.txt
streamlit run app.py
```
